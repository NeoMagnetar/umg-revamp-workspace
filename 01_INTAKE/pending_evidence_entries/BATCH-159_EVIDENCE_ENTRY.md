## Primary Value
This batch supplies a plain-language teaching model for MOLT role separation, especially Trigger, Directive, Instruction, Merge, and Off behavior.

## High-Signal Findings
- MOLT is described as modular cognition rather than static prompting.
- Role definitions are separated clearly enough for beginner documentation.
- Trigger is distinguished from Directive:
  - Trigger detects changed conditions.
  - Directive decides the strategic response.
- Directive appears in two forms:
  - reactive override after Trigger
  - pre-loaded doctrine shaping Instructions before crisis
- Merge refines execution without replacing Primary.
- Off represents stored-but-inactive logic that can later reactivate.

## Main Risks / Ambiguities
- Directive semantics are not yet unified.
- Primary scope may vary across layers, but the source does not formalize how.
- The source is analogy-driven and therefore explanatory rather than schema-stable.

## Recommended Handling
