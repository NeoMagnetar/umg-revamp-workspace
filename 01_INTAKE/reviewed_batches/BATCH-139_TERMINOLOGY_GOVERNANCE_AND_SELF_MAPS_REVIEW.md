## UMG-Relevant Extraction
The inverse table is treated as a reversible lexical mapping rather than a semantic rewrite.
Mappings are required not to alter authority, governance, or resolution semantics.
Self-maps are defined as explicit declarations that a term has been reviewed and accepted unchanged.
A self-map is treated as a valid mapping entry rather than a no-op.
Identity mappings are compatible with reversibility as long as they remain 1→1.

The source strongly reinforces UMG’s broader insistence on explicitness:
even unchanged terms should be declared if they are part of the governed mapping surface.
This keeps lexical translation auditable and machine-readable rather than implicit.

The chat does not redesign MOLT, trigger, priority, merge, or bundle semantics directly.
Instead, it protects those semantics by insisting that terminology translation not drift or smuggle semantic changes.

The documentation/spec-language contribution is substantial:
the source proposes freeze language for a canonical mapping release including:
- name

- frozen status
- invariants
- reversibility requirements
- retired-label absence
- optional stable term IDs
- deprecation ledger

The chat also proposes a Self-Maps Policy section and optional internal tagging such as identity-map metadata, though those remain suggestions rather than frozen canon.

A major workflow contribution is the proposed agent pipeline:

## Independent Review
This batch has high Stage 1 value because terminology discipline is one of the easiest places for semantic drift to enter while appearing harmless.
The self-map clarification is especially important.
Without it, identical-looking terms might be silently treated as outside governance rather than explicitly accepted inside it.

The strongest doctrinal contribution is therefore:
lexical identity still requires explicit review if it participates in a governed reversible mapping system.
That aligns well with broader UMG commitments to visible authority, inspectable structure, and machine-readable control surfaces.

The artifact-compilation portion is also useful because it turns terminology work into a realistic agent/extractor workload rather than leaving it as a static table problem.
That makes the batch relevant not only to docs, but also to future tooling.

Main cautions:
- the source presents the inverse mapping as authoritative in-chat, but does not independently re-derive every mapping from first principles here
- self-map policy was recommended and explained, but not separately frozen as canon
- manifest/schema/tagging ideas remain proposed next-step artifacts, not locked standards
