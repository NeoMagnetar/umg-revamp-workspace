## Independent Review
This batch is unusually important because it does not merely restate the framework. It pressure-tests runtime behavior while simultaneously drafting the formal system needed to support that behavior. The two strongest contributions are:
1. the move toward explicit runtime posture with non-overridable governance continuity; and
2. the shift from descriptive architecture to compiler-verifiable constraint logic.

The most valuable design move is the v2/v3 split. It prevents ontology expansion from destabilizing the hardening work. The most useful concrete artifacts are the dual-layer immersion block and the forensic viewer block, since both are specific enough to drive downstream documentation, compiler, and skill decisions.
