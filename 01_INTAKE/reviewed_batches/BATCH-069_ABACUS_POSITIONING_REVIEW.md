## Independent Review
This batch is valuable, but its value is mainly rhetorical and organizational rather than doctrinal. It sharpens how UMG can be described in contrast to productized AI platforms, especially around transparency, composability, and block-level control. However, several phrases are promotional or interpretive. They should be treated as candidate language, not established ontology.

The strongest durable evidence here is:
- UMG is being understood externally as a modular cognition framework.
- Comparison audiences are likely to ask framework-vs-product questions.
- Documentation and PRD materials will likely need explicit differentiation language.

The main caution is that phrases such as "merge pipelines manually," "ideal for AGI prototyping," and some framework/toolkit wording imply capability or runtime structure without defining it. Those phrases should not be elevated into canon until they are independently reconciled with semantic and compiler decisions.
