## UMG-Relevant Extraction
### 1. Ontology correction
- NeoStacks were treated as domain containers.
- NeoBlocks were treated as grouped intent units.
- MOLT blocks remained the smallest semantic primitives.
- The source explicitly rejects flattening NeoBlocks into atomic units.

### 2. Sleeve architecture
- Sleeves were treated as the full current identity / operating frame rather than style-only overlays.
- A sleeve contains governance, active stacks, defaults, constraints, triggers, weights, merge order, and expression limits.
- Resleeving and cross-model portability were framed as documentation- and architecture-driven, not identity-myth driven.

### 3. Deterministic runtime logic
- Deterministic dominance ordering was tied to weight, axis rank, merge rank, and stable identifiers.
- Trigger routing, escalation, validation, mutation policy, and merge semantics were all moved toward compiler-facing expression.
- Style overlays were repeatedly constrained to surface-only behavior.

### 4. Governance and bounded agency
- Governance was repeatedly placed above sleeves, with sleeves above stacks, stacks above blocks, and expression below intent.
- Tool Anchor Ethos and bounded procedural agency were presented as explicit governance additions.
- Runtime and safety layers reinforced no governance mutation, no unsafe goal generation, bounded improvement, and explicit audit trace.

### 5. Output/render separation
- Format selection was repeatedly described as orthogonal to cognition.
- The source strongly preserves output envelope discipline and presentation-layer separation from execution logic.
