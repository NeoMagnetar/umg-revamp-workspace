## 4. Independent Review
This is strong Stage 1 evidence for sleeve semantics and presentation-layer adaptation. The source is useful because it does two things at once:
1. it clarifies sleeves as modular runtime wrappers rather than permanent identity rewrites, and  
2. it highlights that UMG may need runtime-accessible translation modes when explanation complexity exceeds the user’s comfort threshold.

The main caution is that much of the concrete naming in the chat is illustrative. The named sleeve and overlay labels are valuable evidence, but they should remain provisional until normalized against broader project terminology. The chess mapping is explanatory, not proof of literal runtime mechanics.
