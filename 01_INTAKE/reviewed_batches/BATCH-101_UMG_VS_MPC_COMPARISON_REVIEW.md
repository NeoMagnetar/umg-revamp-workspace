## Independent Review
This batch is best treated as a **comparative semantics and positioning packet**, not an implementation packet. Its highest value is that it makes UMG understandable in relation to a neighboring systems concept—MPC—without collapsing the two into the same thing. That distinction is useful for later public-facing documentation, system comparison sections, and strategic positioning.

The strongest extractable signal is the **Primary / Merge / Blueprint** decomposition. That triad appears here in unusually crisp explanatory language and could become a useful lightweight explanatory surface even if the deeper canonical taxonomy expands later. At the same time, that is also the biggest tension in the batch: later packets introduce broader role inventories, so Stage 1 should preserve this triad as evidence of one framing, not assume it is the final complete taxonomy.

The hybrid architecture idea is also valuable. It suggests a clean conceptual split between **high-level cognitive orchestration** and **low-level control execution**. That could matter for future compiler, agent, robotics, or toolchain integrations. But in this batch, the idea stays conceptual; it does not specify interfaces, scheduling rules, or safety enforcement.

The PDF-export failures materially reduce artifact confidence. This source should be merged as semantic evidence and comparison language, not as proof that a full polished comparative report was successfully preserved.
