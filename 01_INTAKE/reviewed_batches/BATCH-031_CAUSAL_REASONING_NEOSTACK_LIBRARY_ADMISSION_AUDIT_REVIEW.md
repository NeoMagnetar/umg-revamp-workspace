## Chat-Level Summary
The source establishes a practical admission standard for reusable NeoStacks. A stack may be architecturally strong, library-worthy, and still not be compiler-ready if it lacks exact inventories, normalized enums, machine-readable triggers and bundles, formal merge policy, subject arbitration, and validation-grade tests. The reviewed causal-reasoning stack is treated as a strong exemplar with a useful six-block split, but its current artifact is still partly whitepaper-grade rather than runtime-grade.

## UMG-Relevant Extraction
### Core semantic signal
- NeoStacks are reaffirmed as domain-level aggregations of distinct NeoBlocks rather than one oversized reasoning blob.
- Role integrity across Trigger, Directive, Instruction, Subject, Primary, Philosophy, and Blueprint is treated as essential.
- Strong conceptual architecture is not enough for canonical admission without deterministic execution semantics.

### Trigger / activation signal
- Lexical trigger phrases alone are too brittle for deployment.
- Recommended model is two-stage activation: lexical trigger candidates plus intent-classifier or pre-pass confirmation.
- Trigger logic should be normalized into detector refs and machine-readable expressions.
- Triggers are gates, not authority-bearing decision makers.

### Merge / bundle signal
- Merge design is conceptually strong but not compiler-safe yet.
- Missing rules include persistence, downstream reuse, override behavior, dedupe, parent-trigger requirements, activation timing, and traceability.
- Bundle conditions are too informal and should become machine-readable over detector refs, tags, or classifier outputs.

### Neostructure signal
- Six-block split is strong:
  - chain mapping
  - feedback detection
  - root analysis
  - correlation filtering
  - mechanism modeling
  - intervention simulation
- Root + mechanism + intervention is especially valuable because it separates naming a cause, explaining a cause, and testing a cause.
- Stack is suitable for conditional admission after normalization, not immediate deployment.

### Compiler signal
- Required upgrades include exact inventory counts, normalized authority enums, role-precedence rules, detector refs, bundle expressions, merge contract, subject-arbitration mode, and validation-grade tests.
- Source explicitly implies a status ladder: DRAFT_ACCEPTED before COMPILER_READY.
- Source also implies dependence on a PrePass or similar activation-quality layer.

## Independent Review
This is strong Stage 1 evidence for a reusable admission doctrine: preserve good conceptual design, but tighten semantics, counts, and execution rules before canon promotion or runtime deployment. The batch is especially valuable because it does not merely praise a draft; it identifies what differentiates a good reasoning spec from a compiler-safe artifact. The most important caution is that the reviewed stack’s own “ready for deployment” posture is contradicted by unresolved nondeterminism. That contradiction should be tracked, but the source remains highly useful as evidence for future library standards.
