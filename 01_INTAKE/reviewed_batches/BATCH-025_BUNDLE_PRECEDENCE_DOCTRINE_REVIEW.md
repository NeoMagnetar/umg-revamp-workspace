## UMG-Relevant Extraction
### 1. Bundle semantics
- A Bundle is an ordered grouping of same-type MOLT blocks.
- Bundles preserve identity and local order; they do not synthesize a new doctrine.
- Bundles are niche/conditional structures, not a universal default.
- Bundle order is precedence order for the grouped same-type blocks.

### 2. Precedence and exception handling
- Lower-priority blocks operate within the bounds of higher-priority blocks.
- Activation does not erase precedence.
- Exceptions must be declared at or above the level of the rule they qualify.
- Lower operational bundles must not smuggle in moral or doctrinal exceptions absent from higher doctrine.

### 3. Merge vs bundle
- Merge remains rare, high-significance, and synthesis-creating.
- Bundle is not merge; it preserves grouped identity without collapsing distinctions.
- The stricter merge doctrine is explicitly used to avoid an undifferentiated architectural “soup.”

### 4. NeoBlock and sleeve implications
- NeoBlocks may contain standalone MOLT blocks and/or Bundles.
- The dealership sleeve is treated as a stable canon snapshot and future reference sheet.
- The sleeve is more than a title header; it is the enclosing active identity context with defaults, constraints, overlays, and runtime architecture.

### 5. Packaging and release path
- Canon refinement should precede large-scale packaging.
- OpenClaw is treated as the practical near-term adoption path.
- The intended sequence is: stabilize base ontology and doctrine, validate in real sleeves/skills, then scale the library/catalog.

## Independent Review
