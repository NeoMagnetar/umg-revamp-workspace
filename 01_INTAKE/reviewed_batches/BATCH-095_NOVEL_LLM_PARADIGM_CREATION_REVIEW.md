## Independent Review
This batch is valuable because it gives UMG a credible external analogy for modular cognition that is neither purely abstract nor limited to present-day assistant prompt formatting. The source frames modularity as a real systems-design issue: different subsystems handle different representational domains, and an orchestration layer must coordinate them.

The strongest Stage 1 takeaway is that UMG may eventually need explicit semantics for **bridge blocks** or **interoperability blocks**. The source repeatedly returns to the adapter/fusion surface as the mechanism that makes specialized modules work together. That has compiler, documentation, and NeoBlock implications.

The second important takeaway is the distinction between what is practical now and what remains research-grade. That matters for PRD and staging because the batch supplies a clear maturity ladder rather than one undifferentiated future vision.

This source should remain evidence only. It is highly suggestive, but the UMG mappings are mostly analogical and should not be mistaken for approved architecture decisions.
