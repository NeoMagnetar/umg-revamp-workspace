## Chat-Level Summary
This batch tightens the framing of UMG as an externalized cognition system by distinguishing classical deliberation from **constructed deliberation**. Its strongest signal is that **S2′ is a regime of pre-execution, inspectable, portable cognition**, and that **CIR** may be the representation layer through which such cognition is compiled into execution plans. The batch contributes documentation-grade language, diagram language, and positioning vocabulary, but it does not fully resolve how CIR maps onto existing UMG constructs such as blocks, stacks, governance, triggers, merge, bundle, and priority.
