## Independent Review
This is a strong Stage 1 evidence batch because it demonstrates a real construction pipeline rather than only high-level doctrine. The source is especially valuable for showing how poetic or symbolic source material can be converted into explicit UMG architecture without collapsing sleeve identity into pure style. It also contributes a useful design boundary: hard governance layers may remain inherited or external while sleeve-local identity expresses creed, legacy, mythos, or symbolic affinity.

The batch should **not** be promoted directly because several questions are still unresolved: where governance lives, whether mythos has runtime consequences, how QR artifacts relate to canonical registries, and whether these sleeves are meant as formal operational assets or high-affinity authored artifacts. However, the worked examples are valuable for compiler examples, documentation, and future skill workflows.
