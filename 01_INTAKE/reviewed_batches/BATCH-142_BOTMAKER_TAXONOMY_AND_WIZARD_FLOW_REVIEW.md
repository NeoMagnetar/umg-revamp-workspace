## Independent Review
This batch has high Stage 1 value because it provides an early but structured taxonomy for turning bot identity into an implementation-oriented build space.
The strongest contribution is the layered identity model plus tag discipline.
Together they create a plausible bridge from human selection to machine-usable configuration.

The distinction between descriptive labels and executable tags is especially important.
Without it, builder systems drift into flavor-only taxonomy with weak runtime usefulness.
This batch instead moves toward a model where selections can drive filtering, validation, export, and later behavior shaping.

The wizard flow is also strong because it suggests a staged UI/assembly architecture rather than collapsing everything into one giant prompt.
That supports future productization.

Main cautions:
- many categories are candidates rather than canon
- merge/compatibility rules remain implied, not formalized
- tag namespaces are proposed, not schema-enforced
- some role/profession sourcing claims were not independently validated in-chat
- the boundary between general bot-builder product design and UMG-native ontology remains partly open
