# BATCH-017_CANON_CANDIDATES

## Candidate Canon Language
- UMG stops looking like “just a model” and starts looking like a policy-bearing control architecture.
- In defense terms, the decisive question is whether modularity remains bounded, reviewable, and attributable in operational contexts.
- Architecture can provide legal advantage without creating legal sufficiency.
- Near-runtime adaptability is a defense asset only inside a preapproved envelope; outside that envelope, it becomes a compliance problem.
- UMG’s sophistication must remain subordinate to reviewed constraints, human authority, and immutable governance anchors under operational stress.

## Candidate PRD / Staging Inputs
- separate conceptual policy fit from actual fieldability
- stage adoption in bounded, non-kinetic, or decision-support roles first
- treat immutable governance anchors, auditability, and configuration control as release gates
- require strong V&V/OT&E before wider deployment classes

## Candidate Documentation Language
- transparent governance is an assurance feature and a deployment obligation
- runtime modularity is dual-use: operational advantage plus compliance hazard
- bounded adaptation must remain attributable and reviewable
