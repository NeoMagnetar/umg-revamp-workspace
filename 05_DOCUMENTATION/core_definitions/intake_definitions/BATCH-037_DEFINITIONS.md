# BATCH-037 Definitions Extract

## Terminology and Meanings

### cognitive substrate
- The underlying reasoning engine remains the same even when interaction style changes.

### interaction surface
- The visible style, tone, and experiential presentation layer shown to the user.

### stable identity envelope
- The sleeve remains the persistent identity container while posture varies.

### configurable experiential surface
- The user-facing mode can shift through stack configuration without implying engine replacement.

### governance anchor
- The persistent control boundary that remains stable even while surface mode changes.

### identity retention
- A control function ensuring style variation does not become identity drift.

### mirror stack
- A specialized NeoStack used to emulate a target affective or expressive posture more precisely than broad tone adjustment alone.
