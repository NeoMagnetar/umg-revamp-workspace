# BATCH-175 — Definitions Extract

## Terminology Scope Note
These definitions are extracted from a derived canon-building and implementation-planning summary.
They remain Stage 1 evidence until explicitly promoted elsewhere.

## Core Canon Terms
**Cognitive Specification Layer (CSL)**  
A layer that specifies what cognition should exist rather than how cognition runs.

**RuntimeSpec**  
The structured compiled artifact describing what should be active, constrained, allowed, or shaped after UMG compilation.

**Trace**  
The forensic and chronological record of how the final compiled structure was derived.

**PrimaryShell**  
A role describing output-shell participation, explicitly clarified as a role rather than a MOLT type.

## Structural Terms
**Block**  
Atomic unit of cognition; one idea, rule, value, principle, or constraint; inert until compiled.

**Sleeve**  
Static container of potential cognition holding blocks, snaps, defaults, domains, and metadata; not an agent and not runtime state.

**Domain**  
Strong candidate human-facing term for cross-type grouped cognition collections, though not fully finalized in this source.

## Definitional Candidates
UMG is a tech-agnostic Cognitive Specification Layer.  
UMG specifies cognition; execution is external.  
RuntimeSpec + Trace are the primary outputs.  
No Trace = not UMG-compliant.  
Sleeves are lifeless containers of potential cognition.
