# BATCH-017_DEFINITIONS

## Terminology Extract

### Policy-Bearing Control Architecture
- Architectural framing for UMG when governance, auditability, bounded reconfiguration, and explicit operator control become the primary differentiators.
- Stronger than “advanced model behavior”; still not equivalent to legal sufficiency on its own.

### Immutable Governance Anchor
- Control anchor that must remain technically binding and non-bypassable under operational stress.
- Treated as a prerequisite for defense-grade trust.

### Approved Envelope
- Preapproved bounded space inside which runtime adaptation may occur without escaping legal, doctrinal, or command constraints.
- Crossing the envelope converts adaptability from an asset into a compliance problem.

### Legal Advantage
- Benefit gained by explicit policy, transparency, auditability, and bounded control.
- Does not itself prove legal sufficiency or authorize deployment.

### Fieldability
- Practical deployment viability after accounting for testing, review, cybersecurity, acquisition, accountability, and operational control.
