# BATCH-120_DEFINITIONS

## Terminology Extract
- **developmental governance:** a governance model where external constraints limit capability during cognitive immaturity and may become weaker or bypassable as intelligence matures.
- **internalized alignment:** the idea that alignment may eventually be carried by the system’s own reasoning rather than exclusively by external enforced limits.
- **non-self-authorization:** constraint that the system may not authorize its own expansion of authority or permission.
- **cooperative equilibrium:** framing cooperation as a rational long-horizon equilibrium rather than a moral assumption.
- **bounded autonomy:** agent behavior permitted under externally enforced constraints.
- **post-governance behavior:** behavior of a sufficiently advanced system once external governance is no longer enforceable or binding.

## UMG-Relevant Role Language
- **Governance:** what is permitted during developmental phases.
- **Directive:** how alignment is strategically pursued.
- **Philosophy:** cooperative equilibrium / long-horizon lens.
- **Instruction:** constraints such as monotonic rules and no self-authorization.
- **Primary:** prevent misuse during cognitive immaturity.

## Candidate Canon Language
- “Governance is a limiter of power while intelligence matures.”
- “No self-authorization.”
- “Alignment must be structural, not probabilistic.”
- “Cooperation must be rational, not moral.”

## Caution
- This batch does not settle whether governance is permanent or temporary.
- It does not prove that internalized alignment is sufficient.
- It does not establish mathematical dominance of cooperative behavior.
