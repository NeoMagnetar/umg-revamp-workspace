# BATCH-046_DEFINITIONS

## Terminology / Definitional Candidates

### controversy analysis method
- a structured approach that separates what is shown from what is claimed, then layers social and normative analysis on top.

### technical layer
- the directly observable or supportable factual signal in a screenshot, post, or artifact.

### rhetorical layer
- the amplification, framing, emotional coloration, or political charge added around the factual signal.

### social-dynamics layer
- the community frustration, identity alignment, grievance, or emotional momentum surrounding the issue.

### normative synthesis
- the stage where evidence, uncertainty, harms, and benefits are weighed without collapsing distinctions.

### high-variance feature
- a feature with low harm for some adults, meaningful harm risk for others, and unacceptable exposure risk for youth.

### harm-minimized
- framed to reduce foreseeable harm rather than merely take a side.

### consent-forward
- centered on consent boundaries, especially for adult contexts.

### transparency-forward
- explicit about uncertainty, available evidence, and what is not proven.

### ordinary use vs problematic use
- distinction between casual/limited adult use and compulsive, conflict-producing, or harmful use patterns.

### UI-level feature change
- a visible interface or feature-layer change that should not automatically be treated as proof of deeper systemic or governance collapse.
