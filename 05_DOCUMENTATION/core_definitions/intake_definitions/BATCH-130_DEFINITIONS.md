# BATCH-130 — Definitions Extract

## Terminology Scope Note
These definitions are extracted from a derived compiler-spec summary.
They remain Stage 1 evidence until explicitly promoted elsewhere.

## Core System Terms
**UMG**  
A Cognitive Specification Layer that specifies cognition and does not execute it.

**RuntimeSpec**  
Structured, machine-readable result of compilation.

**Trace**  
Forensic log of how the result was produced.

**Block**  
Atomic unit of thought such as an instruction, principle, theme, strategy, or value; inert until compiled.

**Sleeve**  
Static cognitive design container; not an agent, not a prompt, and not an execution surface.

## MOLT and Role Terms
**MOLT**  
Typed grammar defining semantic position, authority order, legal interactions, and conflict behavior.

**Canonical MOLT Order**  
Trigger  
Directive  
Instruction  
Subject  
Primary  
Philosophy  
Blueprint

**Role**  
Participation mode independent of MOLT type.

**PrimaryShell**  
Role indicating container-level output participation.

**MergeContributor**  
Role indicating participation in explicit merge/synthesis contribution.

**BlueprintGuide**  
Silent formatting/stylistic influence role.

**Off**  
Role/state that can apply to any type; not a MOLT type.

## Control and Ordering Terms
**Trigger**  
Named switch or activation logic whose active IDs are supplied externally in v0.

**priorityOrder**  
Ordinal deterministic ordering signal used within allowed levels or ordered structures; not a replacement for authority.

**Authority**  
Determines who may override.

**Priority**  
Determines ordering within an allowed level.

**Governance**  
Law/control layer above operational logic; not a MOLT type.

**GovernanceRule**  
Explicit rule describing governance behavior.

**GovernanceBinding**  
Scoped attachment of governance to Sleeve, NeoStack, Stack, NeoBlock, or block targets.

## Composition Terms
**Bundle**  
Ordered grouping with no new meaning; preserves original meanings, rank, and hierarchy.

**Merge**  
Explicit creation of a new semantic unit with preserved lineage.

**Synthesis**  
Broader constructive process under which Merge operates as a specific primitive.

**Segment**  
Compiler-facing semantic unit, especially bundle segments and merge segments.

**NeoBlock**  
Compressed result containing bundles, merged pieces, singleton items, or structured internals while preserving traceability.

**NeoStack**  
Canonical domain-level grouping of blocks or NeoBlocks whose members are peers by authority.

## Definitional Candidates
No Trace = not UMG.  
Governance is not a MOLT type.  
Bundle = ordered grouping with no new meaning.  
Merge = new meaning with explicit lineage.  
Authority decides who may speak; priority decides who speaks first or louder.  
The compiler never sees naked stacked blocks; it sees bundle/merge segments.
