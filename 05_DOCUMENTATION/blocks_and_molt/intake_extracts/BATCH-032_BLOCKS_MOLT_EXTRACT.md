# BATCH-032 — Blocks / MOLT Extract

## MOLT Surface Under Pressure
The source treats current Subject handling as sometimes too flat for operationally rich tasks. The problem is not general intelligence failure; it is insufficient structured anchoring around purpose, actor/object role, constraints, and utility ordering.

## Proposed Minimal Anchors
- **TG** — terminal goal
- **ROLES** — agent/patient/instrument style semantic role binding
- **ACT** — activation constraint or prerequisite condition
- **U_TARGET** — explicit utility priority ordering

## Optional / Less Settled Additions
- **IG** — instrumental goal
- **SUB_PROFILE** — structured Subject enrichment

## Structural Preference
- do not create many narrow role families
- keep the extension surface small
- use one bounded validation pass instead of ontology expansion

## Interaction With Existing MOLT Thinking
- Subject remains important, but may need support from additional broad anchors
- Priority-like behavior is made more explicit through U_TARGET ordering
- goal coherence becomes a cross-role validation concern rather than an implicit side effect of prose reasoning

## Intake Position
Useful evidence for future MOLT-family alignment, especially where the project must choose between:
- enriching existing roles
- adding a few broad extension anchors
- or moving more responsibility into prepass/resolver layers
