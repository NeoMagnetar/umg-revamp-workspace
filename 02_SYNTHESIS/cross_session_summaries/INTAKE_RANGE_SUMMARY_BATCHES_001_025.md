# INTAKE RANGE SUMMARY — BATCHES 001–025

## Scope
This handoff summarizes the completed Stage 1 intake range from **BATCH-001** through **BATCH-025**.
All material in this range remains **evidence**, not canon.

## Overall Themes
Across this range, the strongest recurring themes were:

1. **Core UMG semantic stabilization**
   - repeated work on sleeves, MOLT, blocks, NeoBlocks, NeoStacks, governance, blueprint separation, and runtime posture
   - repeated attempts to distinguish structural doctrine from presentation, persona, or prompt flavor

2. **Compiler and runtime control**
   - major recurring signals around authority resolution, governance propagation, conflict handling, merge/bundle rules, activation routing, runtime state, and compiler hardening
   - repeated distinction between source assets, compiled outputs, and active runtime state

3. **Sleeve/runtime identity**
   - sleeves repeatedly treated as the active identity/control frame rather than a style layer
   - resleeving, continuity, visible-vs-latent governance, and active-stack visibility appeared often

4. **Output-envelope and formatting separation**
   - many batches focused on the envelope as presentation-only rather than an authority layer
   - repeated tension between heavy explicit structure and usability/copyability

5. **Deployment and packaging**
   - multiple batches pivoted toward practical deployment through OpenClaw / ClawHub / GitHub workflows
   - strong split emerged between neutral tooling, formatter skills, UMG runtime skills, and private homebase repo patterns

6. **Staging discipline**
   - repeated insistence on hardening semantics first, then compiler/runtime contract, then packaging/release
   - repeated separation of Stage 1 evidence handling from later canon/synthesis work

## Most Frequent Domains
The most populated domains across the range were:

- **Core alignment / semantics**
- **Compiler/runtime architecture**
- **Sleeves / runtime identity**
- **Documentation/spec language**
- **Skill/deployment workflow**

## Strongest Signals Across the Range
The strongest recurring signals were:

- **Sleeves define active runtime identity, not just tone**
- **Governance and runtime control must remain explicit, bounded, and non-silent**
- **Output/rendering must stay separate from cognition/control**
- **Compiler logic needs stronger explicit phases rather than hidden winner-picking**
- **Bundle, merge, precedence, and exception doctrine require careful formalization**
- **Deployment should proceed through staged, practical, agent-usable paths**
- **Evidence must remain evidence until explicitly promoted**

## Major Recurring Tensions
The main recurring tensions across the range were:

1. **Visible governance vs latent governance**
   - several batches show that governance may remain conceptually active while its reporting/surface visibility drifts

2. **Priority vs adjudication**
   - older assumptions about hard priority order increasingly gave way to conflict surfacing and adjudication logic

3. **Strict structure vs usability**
   - envelope-heavy, explicit runtime reporting improved inspectability but sometimes degraded copyability, readability, or normal interaction feel

4. **Persona language vs architecture language**
   - several batches worked to move UMG away from persona framing and toward typed cognitive/runtime architecture

5. **Canon refinement vs packaging pressure**
   - repeated pressure to package, deploy, and publish while key doctrine was still being refined

6. **Public description vs internal doctrine**
   - public-facing sleeve language often needed simplification without collapsing deeper distinctions
