# BATCH-076_CONTRADICTION_FLAGS

## Contradiction / Ambiguity Flags

### 1. Governance: executable law vs symbolic mythos
- The source treats governance as always enforced and structurally primary.
- It also includes symbolic/mythic language that may not translate directly into compiler/runtime law.
- This creates ambiguity over what is executable versus narrative.

### 2. Invocation: mention vs execution
- The source explicitly says mention != activation and presence != execution.
- That is useful, but the exact invocation grammar is not formalized.
- Ambiguity remains around what counts as a valid stack-load trigger.

### 3. Merge law: acknowledged but underspecified
- Merge is important and may be directional.
- The actual rule set for directional merge remains open.

### 4. Model-agnostic continuity: principle vs mechanism
- “Structure survives upgrades” is treated as a strong principle.
- The exact reconstruction mechanism across models is only partly described.

### 5. Multi-agent alignment: assumed rather than tested
- Claude, Grok, and GPT-based alignment is described as supported.
- No formal inter-agent synchronization law is specified in this batch.

## Disposition Guidance
These are synthesis-stage contradictions, not reasons to discard the batch. Keep as evidence and route toward canon alignment, compiler clarification, and sleeve formalization.
