# BATCH-153 — Contradiction Flags

## Scope Note
These are not canon decisions.
They are tensions or possible conflicts surfaced by the source.

## Flag 1
**Topic:** Instruction-last rigidity  
**Tension:** Instruction is constrained to last position in V1.  
**Why it matters:** Later richer systems may require multi-stage instruction or post-instruction chaining.

## Flag 2
**Topic:** Trigger minimality  
**Tension:** Primary acts like an implicit trigger, but no explicit trigger primitive exists.  
**Why it matters:** Later runtime systems may need first-class trigger law.

## Flag 3
**Topic:** Merge edge cases  
**Tension:** Merge modes are named, but typing, null handling, and failure behavior are underdefined.  
**Why it matters:** Runtime predictability depends on edge-case semantics.

## Flag 4
**Topic:** Bundle absence  
**Tension:** Stacks act like implicit bundles, but no explicit grouping abstraction is defined.  
**Why it matters:** Packaging and reuse may be harder without bundle law.

## Flag 5
**Topic:** Implicit MOLT mapping only  
**Tension:** The primitive types align structurally with later MOLT ideas, but no explicit taxonomy was declared.  
**Why it matters:** Later synthesis should avoid overstating canon alignment.
