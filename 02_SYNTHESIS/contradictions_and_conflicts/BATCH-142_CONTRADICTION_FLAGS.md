# BATCH-142 — Contradiction Flags

## Scope Note
These are not canon decisions.
They are tensions or possible conflicts surfaced by the source.

## Flag 1
**Topic:** Candidate taxonomy versus canon ontology  
**Tension:** The batch proposes rich builder categories, but the relationship between general bot-builder product taxonomy and UMG-native ontology remains partly open.  
**Why it matters:** Later synthesis should not silently equate product convenience categories with canon structural law.

## Flag 2
**Topic:** Tag namespace proposal without schema enforcement  
**Tension:** Namespace-style tags are proposed, but no validator or formal schema is locked in the chat.  
**Why it matters:** Tooling should not harden around placeholder namespaces before governance review.

## Flag 3
**Topic:** Merge/compatibility logic implied but undefined  
**Tension:** Trait fusion and hybridization are repeatedly suggested, but no precedence, weighting, or conflict-resolution standard is finalized.  
**Why it matters:** Builder flexibility needs explicit compatibility rules before runtime use.

## Flag 4
**Topic:** Step 4 role sourcing  
**Tension:** Professional-role lists were framed as if derived from an external source, but were not actually validated in the chat.  
**Why it matters:** Reference confidence and taxonomy confidence should be kept separate.

## Flag 5
**Topic:** Proposed-final language without ratification  
**Tension:** Some category proposals were described with strong language, but the user did not formally ratify every taxonomy as canon.  
**Why it matters:** Proposal energy should not be mistaken for freeze status.
