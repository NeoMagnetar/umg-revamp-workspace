# BATCH-109 — Contradiction Flags

## Contradiction 1: CSL as Language vs Layer
- **Conflict:** Earlier material described CSL as a language; later material explicitly rejects that.
- **Current safer status:** CSL = Cognitive Specification Layer.
- **Disposition:** Preserve correction. Do not reuse language framing unless a different, explicitly scoped meaning is intended.

## Contradiction 2: CSL Compiles into CIR
- **Conflict:** Earlier formulations said CSL compiles into or emits CIR; later formulations explicitly reject that.
- **Current safer status:** CIR is instantiated and validated within CSL jurisdiction.
- **Disposition:** Mark older “CSL → CIR compilation” language as deprecated.

## Contradiction 3: “Compiler” Terminology After Anti-Compilation Correction
- **Conflict:** The term “UMG Compiler” remains while CSL is declared non-compiling.
- **Current safer status:** Compiler terminology attaches only to UMG-level resolution/binding.
- **Disposition:** Wording discipline required in docs/specs.

## Contradiction 4: Governance Above CSL vs CSL as Decision-Authoritative
- **Conflict:** Governance is placed above CSL, yet CSL is also called decision-authoritative.
- **Current safer status:** Preserve distinction between supreme governance and jurisdictional authority.
- **Disposition:** Needs explicit language to prevent collapse of authority layers.

## Contradiction 5: Static/Live Compilation Language
- **Conflict:** Static/live compilation language appeared even after anti-compilation correction.
- **Current safer status:** Prefer static/live resolution or binding language.
- **Disposition:** Terminology harmonization needed.

## Contradiction 6: Strongly Stabilized Terms vs Underdeveloped Mechanics
- **Conflict:** CSL, UMG, CIR, RuntimeSpec, Trace, PCL, and S2′ are relatively stable in wording, while implementation semantics remain less developed.
- **Current safer status:** Treat this batch as ontology/glossary stabilization, not procedural finalization.
- **Disposition:** Preserve uncertainty and avoid overclaiming execution mechanics.
